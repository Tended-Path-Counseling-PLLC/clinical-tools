Cognitive Defusion
Unhooking from Thoughts
Shifting the client's relationship to thoughts — from fused belief to observed content
Noticing Fusion
When you have that thought, what do you notice happening in your body — and what do you find yourself doing?
Opens the thought-behavior link without challenging content
How long have you had this thought? Is it new, or has it been with you for a while?
When that thought shows up, does it feel like a fact, or does it feel more like something your mind is telling you?
What does your mind say would happen if you didn't act on that thought?
Surfaces the secondary belief driving fusion
Workability
When you follow what that thought is telling you, does it tend to move you toward the life you want — or away from it?
Core workability frame — not "is it true?" but "is it useful?"
What has it cost you to hold onto that story so tightly?
If you believed that thought a little less — not that it's wrong, just less — what might you do differently?
Creating Distance
What if you didn't have to argue with that thought or make it go away — what if you could just notice it was there?
Can you say that thought out loud, but put "I'm noticing I'm having the thought that..." in front of it?
Classic defusion exercise — slows the automaticity of fusion
Where do you think that thought came from? Does it remind you of anything you were taught, or something you heard a lot growing up?
Bridges defusion and inherited values / parts work
Acceptance & Willingness
Making Room
Openness to internal experience without unnecessary struggle or suppression
Exploring Avoidance
What are you doing to try to get rid of that feeling — and how is that working for you over time?
Surfaces the experiential avoidance cycle without pathologizing it
How much energy does it take to keep that feeling at bay?
What do you usually do when that feeling starts to come up? What happens after?
Has trying to not feel that way ever actually made it go away for good?
Introducing Willingness
What would it mean to let that feeling be there — not because you like it, but because fighting it isn't working?
If you knew this feeling wouldn't hurt you — even though it's uncomfortable — would you be willing to let it stay a little longer?
On a scale of 0 to 10, how willing are you right now to make room for that — not to feel good about it, but to stop the war with it?
Window of Tolerance
Right now, where do you notice that feeling in your body — and what does it feel like physically?
Anchors emotional experience somatically before asking for willingness
Can you stay with that sensation for just a moment without trying to change it — just notice it, like you're watching it from the outside?
What's the difference between the feeling itself and the story about what the feeling means?
Present-Moment Awareness
Coming Back to Now
Flexible, non-judgmental contact with the here and now
Noticing Drift
Where did your mind just go? What are you noticing right now?
Gentle re-anchor when the client drifts into past or future
When you think about that situation, are you mostly in the past — replaying it — or in the future — anticipating what might happen?
What's happening for you right now, in this moment — not the story about it, just what's present?
Grounding in Session
Can you take a breath and just notice what you're feeling right here, right now — in your body, in this chair?
If you could set aside the past and the future just for a moment — what's actually true right now?
What do you notice when you slow down and just let yourself be here?
Between-Session Application
When you notice yourself getting pulled into that worry spiral, what could you do to bring yourself back to right now?
What would it feel like to spend even five minutes just being where you are — without your phone, without planning the next thing?
What's one moment this week when you felt actually present — not in your head?
Self-as-Context
The Observing Self
The stable perspective that can witness thoughts and feelings without being defined by them
Introducing the Observer
There's a part of you that's aware that you're having these thoughts and feelings right now. Can you notice that part — the one that's watching?
Direct entry into observer perspective
You've had so many thoughts and feelings over the years — and yet something in you has been there through all of them. What do you make of that?
Who is the "I" that's aware of all of this — the anxiety, the thoughts, the memories? Can you locate that?
Identity and Fusion with Content
You've said "I am anxious" — what's it like to try "I'm noticing anxiety" instead? Does that feel any different?
Languaging shift — from identity fusion to observer stance
If that story about yourself isn't who you are — if it's just a story your mind is telling — who are you underneath it?
Is the part of you that's struggling the same part that's aware of the struggle?
Parts Language (IFS bridge)
It sounds like there's a part of you that believes that, and maybe another part that's not so sure. What does the less certain part say?
Natural bridge from self-as-context into IFS multiplicity
If that inner critic is a part of you — not all of you — what might it be trying to protect you from?
What would it be like to get a little distance from that part — to look at it, rather than through it?
Values Clarification
What Matters
Identifying freely chosen, intrinsic directions — not goals, not rules, not what others expect
Surfacing Values
When you imagine a version of your life that feels genuinely meaningful — not perfect, just real — what's in it?
Open-ended entry; avoids leading toward achievement or outcomes
What do you want to stand for? Not what you want to have or accomplish — but what kind of person do you want to be?
Think of a time when you felt most like yourself — most alive or most right. What was happening? What was present?
What would you regret not having done, or not having been, if you looked back at your life from the end of it?
Useful for clients who struggle to articulate values forward-facing
Distinguishing Values from Rules
When you say that's important to you — does it feel like something you genuinely want, or something you feel like you should want?
Inherited values check — critical for clients with enmeshed family systems
Whose voice does that sound like? Is that coming from you, or from somewhere else?
If no one would ever know — no praise, no judgment — would you still choose that?
Values and Pain
What does the fact that this hurts tell you about what matters to you?
Pain as a values compass — particularly powerful in grief or relational distress
You wouldn't be this hurt if you didn't care. What is it you care about here?
Committed Action
Moving Toward
Building patterns of behavior that carry values into daily life — even in the presence of discomfort
From Values to Action
Given what matters to you, what's one small step you could take this week — not to feel better, but to move toward something that counts?
Values-anchored action — explicitly not contingent on mood or readiness
What would the person you want to be do in this situation?
If anxiety or fear showed up when you tried to do that — and it probably would — could you do it anyway?
Avoidance and Barriers
What gets in the way every time you try to move in that direction? Is it a feeling, a thought, a situation — or some combination?
What would you have to be willing to feel in order to take that step?
Connects committed action back to acceptance — the two processes work together
What's the cost of waiting until you feel ready?
Building Momentum
What's the smallest possible version of that step — something so small it would be hard to talk yourself out of?
Useful when clients feel overwhelmed or perfectionistic about change
How will you know, at the end of this week, that you moved toward what matters — even just a little?
When you did something this week that felt right — something aligned — what did that feel like? Can we build more of that?
